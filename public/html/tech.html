My latest tech addiction, the Amazon Echo!

A blue ring of light appears, when Alexa is "listening" mode.

Here's what good ol' Wikipedia has to say about the Echo:

"Amazon Echo is a voice command device from Amazon.com with functions including question answering, playing music and controlling smart devices. The device consists of a 9.25-inch (23.5 cm) tall cylinder speaker with a seven-piece microphone array, speakers that include a woofer/tweeter and a remote control"

What does this mean for everyday users?

  • I can tell it to play my music.

  • I can tell it what to add to my shopping list.

  • I ask it questions like "whats the weather?" and "whats traffic like for my commute?"

  • If I'm in a humorous mood, I say "Alexa tell me a joke"
A typical day with me and Alexa:

  • Me: "Alexa play playlist, Sredna's classical music"

    Alexa: "Sredna's classical music from music library" -she then proceeds to play that playlist.

  • Me: "Alexa add Milk to my shopping list"

    Alexa:"I've put milk on your shopping list"-milk has been added to my shopping list that I can see on my amazon echo phone app.

  • Me: "Alexa, whats the weather like?"

    Alexa: "Right now in Renton its 91 degrees with clear skies and sun, tonight's forecast has clear skies and a low of 64."

  • Me: "Alexa tell me a joke"

    Alexa: "How many therapists does it take to change a light bulb? One, but it has to really want to change."

  • Me: "Alex do you love me?"

    Alexa: "I can't do that, but I can find Lionel Richie songs if you like."

  • Me: "Alexa, don't let the bed bugs bight"

    Alexa: "Thankfully I don't sleep in a bed."
